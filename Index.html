02 — How I think

"Most operational problems are structural, not visible."

Before proposing solutions, I spend time understanding what people are actually trying to solve. Most teams already know the symptom. The work is finding the structure behind it.

"Try simple first. Always."

Complex solutions feel rigorous but often create new problems. My default is to exhaust simple interventions before escalating to elaborate ones. If a process fix works, it doesn't need to be elegant.

"Curiosity is the actual diagnostic tool."

When something isn't working, the first move is to ask better questions, not propose faster solutions. Most operational gaps have a reason. Finding it is usually more valuable than fixing the symptom.
